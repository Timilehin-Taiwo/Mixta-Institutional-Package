Sales Execution Playbook

The non-negotiable 6-step process and universal objection handling[cite: 543, 597].

The 6-Step Process [cite: 543]

  • Identify your door opener: Always target HR: Head of HR, HR Director, Chief People Officer, or Head of Employee Benefits. Do not go to the CEO first[cite: 546, 547].
  • Make first contact: Match the culture. Shell needs a formal letter; Flutterwave needs a Linkedin DM. Mismatching culture signals incompetence[cite: 553, 554].
  • Send a teaser before the meeting: WhatsApp or email a one-page summary. Maximum 5 bullet points. No long PDFs[cite: 556].
  • Present the dual pitch (30 minutes): Lead with the Staff Offer (equity installments + cash refund + appliances). This creates excitement[cite: 559].
  • Leave a commitment form (EOI): Do not leave without placing a physical or digital Expression of Interest form. It collects name, phone, monthly income bracket, and interest level to get 10 names[cite: 564, 565, 566].
  • Follow up within 48 hours: Offer to run an in-office Staff Lunch Briefing — a 45-minute session where you present directly to eligible staff[cite: 569].

Universal Objection Handling [cite: 597]

"We already have a mortgage scheme." [cite: 598]
This is not a competing product it is a housing benefit layer[cite: 598]. The cash refund and appliance grant sit on top of any existing scheme[cite: 598].
"How do we know the properties are legitimate?" [cite: 598]
Full documentation: Certificate of Occupancy, development approvals, offtake agreements, and references from institutional partners. Site visits available[cite: 598].
"This sounds too good to be true." [cite: 601]
"The cash refund is funded from the bulk offtake discount our developer extends to us for bringing 10+ buyers at once"[cite: 601]. The appliance grant comes out of the same pool[cite: 601].
