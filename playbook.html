Sales Execution Playbook

The non-negotiable 6-step process and universal objection handling[cite: 471, 500].

The 6-Step Process [cite: 471]

  • Identify your door opener: Always target HR. Do not go to the CEO first[cite: 472].
  • Make first contact: Match the culture (e.g., Shell needs a formal letter; Flutterwave needs a LinkedIn DM)[cite: 474].
  • Send a teaser before the meeting: WhatsApp or email a one-page summary[cite: 476].
  • Present the dual pitch (30 mins): Lead with the Staff Offer (equity installments + cash refund + appliances)[cite: 478].
  • Leave a commitment form (EOI): It collects name, phone, monthly income bracket, and interest level to get 10 names[cite: 481, 482].
  • Follow up within 48 hours: Offer to run a 45-minute Staff Lunch Briefing where you present directly to eligible staff[cite: 484].

Universal Objections [cite: 500]

"We already have a mortgage scheme." [cite: 501]
This is not a competing product — it is a housing benefit layer[cite: 501]. The cash refund and appliance grant sit on top of any existing scheme[cite: 502].
"How do we know the properties are legitimate?" [cite: 506]
Full documentation: Certificate of Occupancy, development approvals, offtake agreements, and references from institutional partners[cite: 506].
"This sounds too good to be true." [cite: 511]
"The cash refund is funded from the bulk offtake discount our developer extends to us for bringing 10+ buyers at once"[cite: 511].
