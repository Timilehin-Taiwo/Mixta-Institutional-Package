Industry Targeting Frameworks

Sector-specific entry logic, behavioral traits, and conversational levers.

1. Financial Services (Banking)

Entry logic: Go to HR, never to Procurement or the CEO. Bank HR Directors own staff welfare budgets and have the authority to endorse external employee benefit programmes.

Sector lever: Banking sector staff are financially literate; they respond to structured financial logic. Present the DSR calculation. Show the numbers.

2. Telecoms & Technology

Entry logic: LinkedIn DM is the correct channel for fintech and tech (Flutterwave, Paystack, SystemSpecs). Formal letters are unnecessary and signal misaligned culture.

Sector lever: Tech staff are financially sophisticated and data-driven. They respond to investment-grade language: ROI, capital appreciation, NPV.

3. Oil, Gas & Energy

Entry logic: International companies (Shell, Total Energies) require formal documentation and due diligence packs. Begin with a formal letter and C of O evidence.

Sector lever: Oil & Gas professionals have high incomes but are acutely aware of Nigerian property title risk. The Certificate of Occupancy on both Ibudo Wura and Beechwood Park II is the single most powerful credential with this audience.

4. FMCG & Manufacturing

Entry logic: Use formal letters for Nestlé, Unilever, and Flour Mills — all structured HR environments.

Sector lever: FMCG HR Directors are measured on retention and welfare metrics. Frame this as a measurable staff benefit.

5. Professional Services

Entry logic: Big Four firms are governed by partnership structures. Reach the People & Culture lead on LinkedIn, send a structured document, and request a 30-minute slot.

Sector lever: Consultants and investment professionals analyse deals for a living. Do not over-pitch — present the structured financial case and let them interrogate it.
