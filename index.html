03 Industry Targeting

Sector Frameworks

Each sector demands a different entry logic, lever, and language.

Financial Services — Banking
10 companies
Entry Logic
Go to HR, never to Procurement or the CEO. Bank HR Directors own staff welfare budgets and have authority to endorse external employee benefit programmes. Procurement will kill the conversation by treating it as a vendor relationship.
Sector Lever
"Your Band 6–9 Officers and Managers earn ₦1.8M–₦7M but are renting in Lekki, Ajah, and Ikoyi. The cash-back programme and equity instalment plan convert that income into an asset — at zero cost to the bank." Banking staff are financially literate. Show the DSR calculation. Present the numbers.
Engagement Sequence — Do Not Pitch All 10 Simultaneously
Each closed deal becomes the reference case for the next. "We just activated this for 15 Access Bank staff — let me show you what their HR team did." Social proof within the sector is the compounding mechanism.

Telecoms & Technology
6 companies
Entry Logic
LinkedIn DM is the correct channel for fintech/tech (Flutterwave, Paystack, SystemSpecs). Formal letters are unnecessary and signal misaligned culture. For MTN and Airtel, use LinkedIn + phone — larger HR teams but still digitally accessible.
Sector Lever
Tech staff are data-driven. Lead with investment-grade language: ROI, capital appreciation, NPV. "Your salary can buy a ₦50M asset today that will be worth ₦100M–₦115M in 5 years at current appreciation rates. The cash-back reduces your effective entry cost." Show the compound growth chart.
Key Sensitivity — Career Mobility
Tech workers are career-mobile. Pre-empt this proactively: "Even if you relocate, you own a Lagos asset yielding 8–10% per annum in rental income. It is your hedge." Turn mobility from an objection into a buying argument before it is raised.
Oil, Gas & Energy
5 companies
Entry Logic
International companies (Shell, TotalEnergies) require formal documentation and due diligence packs. Begin with a formal letter and C of O evidence. Expect legal review requests — welcome them, prepare for them in advance.
Primary Credential
The Certificate of Occupancy on both Ibudo Wura and Beechwood Park II is the single most powerful credential with this audience. Lead with it. Follow with the financial model. Close with the instalment plan.
The Three Risk Elimination Frame
"Three risks keep O&G professionals from buying Lagos property: (1) lump-sum equity → eliminated by instalment plan · (2) title insecurity → eliminated by C of O · (3) post-move fitout cost → eliminated by ₦500K appliance grant. This programme removes all three simultaneously." Frame every pitch around risk elimination.
FMCG, Consumer Goods & Manufacturing
4 companies
Entry Logic
Use formal letters for Nestlé, Unilever, and Flour Mills — all structured HR environments. Nigerian Breweries responds to formal channels with Heineken parent context. All four require documented approval chains — never shortcut this.
Sector Lever
FMCG HR Directors are measured on retention and welfare metrics. Frame as a measurable staff benefit: "Financial wellness: homeownership participation rate." Give them language for their HR dashboard. "In 3 months, X% of your managers moved from renting to owning — a welfare outcome reportable to the global parent."
Strength Angle — Tenure as Creditworthiness
FMCG managers have 7–15 year average tenure — making them ideal mortgage borrowers with consistent income and low default risk. This is a selling point with the HR Director: "Your long-tenure managers are the most creditworthy professionals in Lagos. This programme was built for them."
Professional Services & Financial Advisory
5 companies
Entry Logic
Big Four firms are governed by partnership structures. Reach the People & Culture lead on LinkedIn, send a structured document matching their deck standards, and request a 30-minute slot at the next Talent team meeting. Coronation: direct CEO/CHRO via phone.
Sector Lever — Let Them Interrogate
Consultants analyse deals for a living. Do not over-pitch. Present the structured financial case and let them interrogate it. "Here is the investment thesis. Here is the DSR. Here is the C of O. Here is the cash-back schedule. What questions do you have?" Confidence in the structure is the sale.
Multiplier Effect — Cross-Sell Opportunity
Big Four professionals advise corporate clients on HR strategy. An early, satisfied cohort of Big Four staff becomes an advocacy channel into their corporate clients. Every satisfied Big Four homeowner is a potential referral into a future target company. This is a pipeline multiplier — protect it.

Identify Your Door Opener
Always target HR: Head of HR, HR Director, Chief People Officer, or Head of Employee Benefits/Total Rewards.

Do not go to the CEO first. Do not go to Procurement. Go to HR. The entry point for every company in this document is specified in Section 02 (Pipeline). The entry contact for each company has been pre-identified — use it.
Procurement will classify this as a vendor relationship and route it to procurement committee. HR owns the outcome. One call to the wrong person costs you 3 weeks.
Make First Contact
Each company has a specified contact method in the Pipeline section. Match the culture: Shell needs a formal letter; Flutterwave needs a LinkedIn DM. Mismatching culture signals incompetence and erodes trust before you have it.

Goal of first contact: one meeting. Nothing else. Do not pitch on first contact. Do not send a 40-page PDF. Request the room.
Subject line benchmark: "Staff Housing Programme — Zero Cost to [Company Name]" — this one line removes the procurement reflex before they've even opened it.
Send a Teaser Before the Meeting
The day you confirm the meeting: WhatsApp or email a one-page summary. Maximum 5 bullet points. No long PDFs.

A concise teaser signals preparation and gives them something to share internally before you arrive. The goal is to have the HR Director say "I showed this to my colleague and they want to be in the room."
The teaser circulates internally before you arrive. It is your silent advocate in the organisation. Treat it like a product.
The Dual Pitch — 30 Minutes
Lead with the Staff Offer (equity instalments + cash refund + appliances). This creates the excitement. Then pivot to the Institutional Offer (Lakowe Lakes retreat discount) only if necessary to maintain momentum.

End every presentation with the activation question: "Do you see 10 people in this company who would benefit from this?"
Never lead with the retreat discount. It is a secondary lever deployed to maintain momentum — not your headline. If you open with it, you become a hospitality vendor, not a housing programme.
Leave a Commitment Form (EOI)
Do not leave without placing a physical or digital Expression of Interest form. Ask them to circulate it internally.

The form collects: name, phone, monthly income bracket, and interest level. It is not a legal commitment — it gets 10 names. 10 names activate the full offer. Make this framing explicit with the HR Director.
Phrase it as: "This form is just a show of hands — no liability, no commitment. It's how we know who to invite to the lunch briefing." Remove every reason to hesitate.
Follow Up Within 48 Hours
Offer to run an in-office Staff Lunch Briefing — a 45-minute session where you present directly to eligible staff. This is the close meeting. The lunch briefing is where 10 commitments happen.

Offer to cover the lunch cost. It is a sales cost, not a goodwill gesture.
If they ask for time: leave the EOI form and set a specific follow-up date before you leave the room — not "I'll be in touch." A date is a commitment. "I'll be in touch" is a disappearance.
→ Core Pitch Scripts — Memorise These
Opening — First 2 Minutes
"I'm here because we've built a housing benefit your company can offer to staff completely free of charge. No liability, no cost to you, nothing to sign today. I just want to show you what your eligible managers could access in the next 90 days."
If sceptical: "I know that sounds too good to be true — let me show you the numbers first. May I have 3 minutes on screen?"
Equity Instalment Plan
"A manager earning ₦2M a month can own a ₦50M Ibudo Wura home. The 10% equity — ₦5 million — is spread over 9 months: ₦556,000 per month. Less than most managers' current rent in Lekki."
Adjust instalment illustration to the specific income band of each company's target cohort.
The Cash Refund — Stress "Cash"
"Once your staff member completes their equity payment and the mortgage is in place, we pay them 3 full months of mortgage payments in cash. Not a voucher. Not a credit. Cash. Deposited into their account."
Stress "cash" repeatedly. It is the most powerful word in this pitch. Every time.
Appliance Grant
"On top of that: ₦500,000 in home appliances — air conditioning, gas cooker, washing machine, and heat extractor — fully fitted at move-in. They do not unpack into an empty house. They unpack into a home."
"They move in. Everything is there." End this beat with this line every time.
Institutional Offer — Secondary Lever Only
"As a secondary benefit: whenever your company needs to host a corporate retreat, your HR team gets 3–5% off the full cost at Lakowe Lakes Golf and Country Estate. Minimum 2 nights, up to 5 times a year."
Only deploy this if momentum has stalled. Do not lead with it — the staff offer is the primary product.
The Close — One Question
"I am not asking you to sign anything today. One question: do you see 10 people in this company who earn ₦1.8M or above, are currently renting in Lagos, and would want to own a home in the next 6 months?"
If yes: schedule the lunch briefing before you leave the room. If they ask for time: leave the EOI form and set a specific date for follow-up.
→ Universal Objection Handling
We already have a mortgage scheme.
This is not a competing product — it is a housing benefit layer. The cash refund and appliance grant sit on top of any existing scheme. Think of it as a staff subsidy, not a competing loan.
We need HQ or Management approval.
Understood. We prepare a one-page brief for your MD or CHRO framing this as an endorsed staff housing programme. There is no financial commitment for the company — staff participate individually. You are the facilitator.
How do we know the properties are legitimate?
Full documentation: Certificate of Occupancy, development approvals, offtake agreements, and references from institutional partners. Site visits available — staff see the homes before committing.
Our staff already own homes.
We target staff who are renting — we identify them specifically. Even in senior populations, 65–80% of Lagos professionals at ₦1.8M–₦5M are still renting. The EOI form self-selects.
This sounds too good to be true.
Let me show you the numbers. The cash refund is funded from the bulk offtake discount our developer extends for bringing 10+ buyers at once. The appliance grant comes from the same pool. The economics work because volume justifies the incentive.
We need to review this legally.
We welcome it. We have a full legal due diligence pack: C of O, development permits, title search, and reference letters from existing institutional partners. Shall we prepare a pack specifically for your legal team?
Staff might not want to live in Lakowe.
Lakowe is 25–35 minutes from Victoria Island via the Lekki–Epe Expressway — closer to Lagos' economic centres than Ajah, Sangotedo, or Ibeju, where many qualifying staff currently rent. We include a commute analysis in every briefing.
